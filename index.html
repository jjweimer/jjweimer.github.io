About Me

I’m a recent graduate of the University of California San Diego where I studied political science and data analytics. I enjoy using quantitative methods to solve social science problems, and aspire to have a career using data to effect positive change in my community. During my undergraduate studies I worked on a research project with professor Michael Joseph which aims to understand how U.S. military intervention effected Afghan quality of life. I also worked at the UCSD Data & GIS Lab where I developed web apps for personal insights and automated external reporting of library service statistics data.

In my freetime I love to play chess and train for triathlons. I also have organized and competed in Rubik’s Cube competitions and achieved a ranking of 11th in the world (more on that below!).
